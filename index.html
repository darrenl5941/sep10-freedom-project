The Stock Market

The stock market is a bunch of stocks, or shares of companies that you can buy and sell. The value of those stocks constantly varies as they depend on how the companies are doing and other factors. Trading stocks requires a lot of technology to work and is needed for peope to manage stocks properly. For example, such as stock market apps can help people to buy and sell stocks while monitors can help manage them. There is also peices of hardware which can help make trading stocks faster or make it easier to coordinate trading stocks with others.

Hardware in the Stock Market

Montors

Stock Traders will use monitors to watch if the value of any stocks they have change so they know if they should buy or sell them.

An average sized monitor can usually comfortably hold around 3 or 4 graphs or charts at once without them being too small to see. Because of this some traders will use multiple monitors so they can keep track of more stocks and metrics at once without having to swap tabs or constantly change which ones they are viewing on their screen.

The Asus ProArt PA328CGV (32-inch)

This monitor is good for beginner and intermediate traders because it has a quick refresh rate of 165Hz, allowing for stock values to be updated faster, while having a relativley low price.

The Samsung Odyssey CRG9

This monitor is better for those more invested into stocks as it is very wide with a screen ratio of 32:9, allowing for a lot of stocks to be watched at once

The Acer SB220Q bi (21.5-inch)

This is a good option for people with a very low budget because it only costs $100.

Advanced Hardware in the Stock Market

Quantum Devices

Quantum Devices allows for nearly instant and coordinated buying or selling of stocks which is extremely useful for groups of stock traders.

Field-Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs)

An FPGA is a computer chip with low latency, high performance, and energy efficiency that can be coded to do whatever is needed, in the case of stock traders it can allow for specialized trading algorithms to make trades using new information, and make sure the trades are fair.
